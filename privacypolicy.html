Privacy Policy

Chromlab respects the privacy of its customers, visitors, and partners and is committed to protecting personal information in a lawful, fair, and transparent manner. This Privacy Policy explains how Chromlab collects, uses, stores, and safeguards information when you visit its website, communicate with the company, or purchase laboratory and medical equipment. It applies to all online and offline interactions with Chromlab, unless a separate policy is provided.

Chromlab collects personal information that you voluntarily provide, such as your name, company name, designation, email address, phone number, billing and shipping address, tax identification details, and any information shared in enquiries, quotation requests, or support tickets. In addition, technical data such as IP address, browser type, device identifiers, pages visited, and access times may be collected through cookies or similar technologies to improve website functionality, security, and user experience.

The information collected is used to process and fulfil orders, provide quotations, respond to enquiries, manage customer accounts, offer technical support, and communicate about products, services, and updates relevant to your interests. Chromlab may also use data for internal purposes such as analytics, service improvement, fraud prevention, and compliance with legal and regulatory obligations. Marketing communications are sent only where permitted, and you may opt out at any time using the unsubscribe options provided or by contacting Chromlab directly.

Personal information is shared only to the extent necessary. This may include sharing with logistics providers, payment processors, IT and hosting service providers, or other professional advisers who support Chromlab operations, under appropriate confidentiality and data protection obligations. Chromlab does not sell personal data to third parties. Information may be disclosed if required by law, court order, or competent authorities, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Chromlab, its customers, or third parties.

Chromlab implements reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure. These measures may include access controls, encryption where appropriate, secure servers, regular monitoring, and staff awareness and confidentiality commitments. Personal information is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, to meet legal, accounting, or reporting requirements, or to resolve disputes. When data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymized.

You may have certain rights under applicable data protection laws, which may include the right to access, correct, update, or delete your personal information, to restrict or object to certain processing, and to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. Requests to exercise these rights can be made by contacting Chromlab through the details provided on its website. Chromlab will review and respond to such requests in accordance with applicable law.

Chromlab may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in legal requirements, technology, or business practices. The latest version will always be available on the company’s website, with the effective date indicated at the top. By continuing to use Chromlab services or website after changes are published, you acknowledge and agree to the updated policy. For any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or Chromlab’s handling of personal information, you are encouraged to contact the company using the contact information available on the website.
